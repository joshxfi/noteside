---
title: Wikilinks & backlinks
description: Link notes together with [[wikilinks]] and see what points back.
---

Noteside links notes with `[[wikilinks]]`, the same syntax you may know from
Obsidian or a personal wiki.

## Writing a link

Type `[[` and an autocomplete menu offers your notes by title. Pick one and you get a
`[[Target]]` link. Links are matched against the literal text — Noteside never
rewrites your `[[ ]]` into hidden markup, so vim motions and selections behave exactly
as they look on the line.

## Following a link

Put the cursor on a `[[link]]` and:

- press `gf` (vim), or
- run `:follow`

to open the note it resolves to. Resolution is deterministic and tries, in order:

1. an exact **title** match,
2. an exact **filename** match,
3. the **filename slug**,
4. the **title slug**.

Empty or punctuation-only targets never match, so a stray `[[]]` won't jump anywhere.

## Backlinks

To see everything that links _to_ the current note, open the **Backlinks** panel:

- `⌘⇧L`, `:backlinks` (`:back`), or `<Space> l`

The scan runs in Rust over the cached notebook, so only the references cross into the
UI — it stays fast even on large notebooks.

<Callout>
  Backlinks are computed from the notes as they are on disk. Because links live in the plain text,
  they keep working if you edit, grep, or sync your notebook from outside Noteside.
</Callout>
